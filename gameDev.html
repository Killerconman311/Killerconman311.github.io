Chase The Moon

Chase the Moon is a VR Rogue-Lite set in the clouds above Earth. I developed this game from the ground up using Unity and C#. In Chase the Moon, players find themselves battling the Moon Monk and their minions using a wand and spellbook. The game leverages gesture recognition for casting spells and features a nearly fully diegetic UI. Designed to be punishing yet fair, the mechanics require players to master dodging, blocking, and spell-casting to survive the Moon Monk's onslaught.

 

Entangled

Entangled is a captivating story where love defies the laws of time and space, while also making the concept of quantum entanglement easy to understand. This tale takes the form of an interactive comic book that leads you on a cosmic journey, following the lives of two shape-like characters. As the story progresses, you'll learn more about the basic concepts of quantum entanglement without any fuss. By the end, you'll discover the surprising truth: the lovers are particles connected across the vastness of space. Through storytelling and interactive design, we've made a complex idea simple and fun to explore, so everyone can enjoy the wonders of quantum physics on pc, android, and IOS. Continue on to get a look at the challenges of writing the software for a project like this.

 

From my very first brainstorm I knew that it was going to be important for users to immediately interact with quantum principles the second they opened the app. Interactive experiences are infinitely more rewarding when users make discoveries on their own, so I wanted to show, not tell.

Concept Product

I fell in love with the idea the user being able to touch and drag the particles around the screen. My hope was that before we revealed the true nature of the story, the user would have already interacted with the particles in a way that would make the reveal feel like a "Eureka!" moment.

 

We landed on using the main characters instead of actual sub-atomic particles to avoid being to on the nose with the subject matter. The characters (representing the particles) instantiate randomly, and upon being touched, immediately force their counterpart to mirror their own movement. This was a fun way to introduce the concept of entanglement without directly telling the user "quantum entanglement is when two particles are connected across space and time." The end goal is for the user to drag the particle-characters into the hit-box of the sleeping character in the middle 3 times, after which the main character wakes up from a dream in class, and the story begins.

 

Raven

"Raven" was my first dive into game-making. With Unity and C#, I cooked up this 2D platformer starring Raven the cat. The player leaps between giant cat trees & moving cloud-platforms while collecting fish. I dreamed up and created this pixelated adventure myself. Games like "Celeste" and "Ori" inspired me to make a movement system that felt buttery, but with some mechanics that felt fun to learn and difficult to master. With the surge in popularity of Indie pixel-art games, I knew wanted to try my hand at creating some of my own sprites. That ambition led me to create every asset you see from scratch in Photo-shop. The trailer that accompanies this description was an assignment for an audio/video class in which we were tasked with making our first-ever (oftentimes janky) games look convincingly fun. After you watch it, you will see that "Raven" isn't just a game; it's the spark that lit my passion for digital creation.
